Ethical issues relating to the technology, your personal reflections and recommendations

[Note: Your individual and unique perspective on the topic/issue].


Technology and especially computer systems and networks are increasingly becoming a part of the fabric of our individual lives. For our everyday lives,
interactions with government, business, leisure, personal information, leisure, study and certification and basically any area you can think of, we are
using computer information systems to do what we do. Technology is growing and changing and in turn changing our lives, faster than lawmakers and
security professionals are able to react. The risks to governments, businesses, organistations of all kinds and individuals is greater than ever
and it is only getting more dire each year that passes.

Ethics in cyber security has to do with securing data, networks and systems; in order to defend the human interests that depend on them.
Computer systems have direct bearing on human lives. The recent Waikato DHB cyber attack is a clear example of an information theft and
ransom attack on a computer system which caused patient appointments to be cancelled and hospital systems shut down directly endangering
individuals health outcomes and privacy. If those responsible did not take adequate security measures “to protect its information systems,
it could be liable to any staff member, contractor or patient who suffers harm as a result”.

With large companies like Google and Facebook having such a large influence in our lives, ethical considerations reach into the promotion
or obfuscation of information available to individuals, censorship issues of free speech and disinformation and election campaign issues as
we have recently seen with the 2016 US election for example.

I think since the high monetary and performance costs of implementing adequate security are a problem, and since governements
are struggling to tackle cyber security threats; there is a feeling of recklessness and chaos in the world of computer technology and security.
Malicious actors are making lots of money from attacks, and individuals and organisations suffer huge losses. I think at this time governments
of the world need to continue to work together and allocate more of their money and efforts into catching criminals and creating more effective
legal partnerships and structures.

To me it seems that individual countries don’t stand much of a chance against such a truly global problem. I also get the feeling that the
responsibility is on individuals and organisations to defend themselves against threats they can not reasonably defend themselves against,
which looks like victim blaming to me. I think there need to be proper chains of ethical responsibilty and accountability, through much more
powerful national cyber security agencies working within government and police systems combined with regulations or licenses for software
and hardware for example making hardware firewalls mandatory or forcing operating system providers to certify system safety.
Ultimately I don’t know much about it but I’d like to learn more.
